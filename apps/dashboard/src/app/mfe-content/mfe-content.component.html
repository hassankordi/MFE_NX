Micro Frontends Overview!

Imagine a large shopping mall where each store operates independently but provides a seamless experience for visitors. Micro Frontends work similarly, breaking a large application into smaller, independently developed parts that come together smoothly.

___ Mirroring the principles of microservices on the backend.

Why Use Micro Frontends?

  • Makes development faster and easier.
  • Each part can be built and updated separately.
  • Teams can work independently without affecting the entire application.
  • Improves performance and scalability.

Why Micro Frontends Matter ?

As applications grow, maintaining a single codebase becomes more challenging. The benefits of micro frontends include:

  1. Scalability: Large teams can work in parallel without bottlenecks, ideal for distributed teams.
  2. Faster Development & Deployment: Teams can release updates independently without dependencies on other parts.
  3. Code Maintainability: Smaller codebases improve management, testing, and debugging with better modularity.
  4. Technology Flexibility: Teams can choose the best tools and frameworks for their needs.
  5. Independent Deployment: Each micro frontend can be deployed separately, reducing risks and allowing faster rollbacks or updates without affecting the entire application.

Challenges of Micro Frontends ?

Although micro frontends solve many issues of scalability and maintainability, they introduce new challenges:

  1. Performance Issues: More network requests, larger bundle sizes, and possible code duplication.
  2. Inconsistent UI: Hard to maintain a uniform design across different micro frontends.
  3. Team Coordination: Changes affecting multiple parts require strong collaboration.
  4. Complex Deployment: Managing separate deployments can be challenging.
  5. Limited Community Support: Fewer resources and best practices compared to traditional architectures.
